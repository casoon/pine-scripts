# Wolfe Wave Scanner Pro

Searches a depth window of confirmed swings for every distinct, non-overlapping 5-point Wolfe Wave wedge — a contracting, alternating channel where point 5 tests the 1-3 trendline — not just the single highest-scoring one. Each pattern found projects its own EPA (Estimated Price at Arrival) target from line 1-4 at the time lines 1-3 and 2-4 converge, and is tracked live independently until price confirms or invalidates it.

Wolfe Waves are strongly interpretation-dependent — small changes in swing strength or search window shift which geometry gets found. Treat this as a **structural setup finder with a quality score**, not a signal machine: "pattern found" means "a Wolfe-shaped geometry exists here, worth checking against context," not "enter now."

## Features

- Alternating swing engine (`ta.pivothigh`/`ta.pivotlow`) with bounded history
- Best-of-window search: every valid point-5 anchor within `Suchbereich letzte Swings` is tried (a Wolfe wave is often already several swings old by the time it completes), and for each, points 1-4 are chosen combinatorially from all valid non-consecutive swings — a wave spanning e.g. swings 1,3,5,7,9 is evaluated too, not just the literal last 5 swings
- Tracks up to `Max. gleichzeitige Patterns` distinct, non-overlapping valid patterns at once, each with its own drawings and invalidation state — a second genuinely separate Wolfe wave elsewhere on the chart is no longer discarded just because it scores lower than the first one found. An overlapping candidate only replaces an already-tracked pattern at that spot if it scores higher
- Weighted 0-100 score across eight criteria: structure, contraction + slope consistency, point-4 channel containment, apex convergence, point-5 trendline test, reward (capped to a sane ATR distance), timing, and leg-time symmetry
- EPA target is the price on line 1-4 at the apex time (where lines 1-3 and 2-4 converge, both drawn bold and stopping exactly there — no dangling infinite extension). The 1-4 line itself is drawn visibly through points 1 and 4 up to that same apex bar, and the vertical apex marker drops from that exact point to point 5 — one shared point everywhere, label/dashboard/drawing included
- Live invalidation tracking: once a pattern fires, a break beyond the point-5 ± 0.5 ATR level marks it invalidated and fires an alert — `Invalidierung nur per Close` (default on) chooses between a confirmed close beyond the level or an intrabar high/low breach
- Near-miss candidate marker (`Watch` signal, visual only, requires a real bull/bear structure) plus a dashed candidate wedge (same 1-3/2-4 boundaries as a real pattern) and a score-breakdown debug table for tuning thresholds
- Light-theme dashboard (status, best score, bias, EPA, invalidation level) — Bias/EPA/Invalidation reflect the best currently *tracked* pattern when one exists, the raw search candidate otherwise
- `Invalidierung anzeigen` (default off) draws the invalidation level line itself; invalidation *tracking* (the pattern still gets removed/alerted when the level breaks) always runs regardless of this toggle
- Optional `Reward als Pflichtfilter` (default off) to hard-require a minimum reward instead of only scoring it

## Scoring

| Criterion | Points | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | 30 | Core, non-negotiable shape: sequence alternates high/low and point 3 undercuts/exceeds point 1, point 5 extends beyond point 3 (lows declining for a bull setup, highs rising for bear) |
| Contraction + slope | 12 | Channel (line 2-4 vs. points 1/5) narrows by at least 30% end-to-start *and* narrows monotonically through the midpoint (no bulging outward around points 2-4); the 1-3 and 2-4 boundaries trend the same direction *and* their slope magnitudes are within a 3x ratio of each other (rules out a near-flat line paired with a near-vertical one) |
| Channel containment | 8 | Point 4 sits between points 1 and 2 — a shape nicety, scored separately so it can't zero out `Structure` on its own |
| Convergence (apex) | 15 | Lines 1-3 and 2-4 converge ahead of point 5, within `2×` the pattern's own width |
| Point-5 zone | 15 | Point 5 sits within `p5TolAtr` ATR of the 1-3 trendline (symmetric distance, either side) |
| Reward | 10 | Line 1-4 evaluated at point 5's own bar (not the EPA/apex time) lies beyond point 5 in the expected direction, and isn't further away than `Max. Zielentfernung ATR-Multiple` — an internal-only check on point 5's overshoot, distinct from the EPA target itself |
| Timing | 5 | Legs are monotonically spaced, the final leg isn't disproportionately long relative to the rest, and no single leg (including a middle one) consumes more than 40% of the pattern's total duration |
| Symmetry | 5 | Corresponding legs (1-2 vs. 3-4, 2-3 vs. 4-5) don't diverge too far in duration |

A pattern only fires when its swing chain actually forms the required bull/bear structure (the 30-point `Structure` criterion), the 1-3 boundary was never broken prematurely (`Boundary Hold`, below), point 5 itself is a genuine test of that boundary rather than blowing through it (`P5-Zone`, gated — see below), the wedge apex actually converges ahead of point 5 (`Convergence`, gated — see below), *and* the weighted score clears `Mindestqualität` — `valid` requires all five explicitly, so the other criteria reaching the score threshold on their own (they can, structure-agnostic points alone can add up past a low `Mindestqualität`) still can't unlock a signal for a chain that isn't a real Wolfe sequence. `Reward als Pflichtfilter` (off by default) optionally adds `Reward` to that list of hard requirements too, for users who'd rather reject a formally valid Wolfe wave with a poor risk/reward than just lose its 10 points.

**Boundary Hold (veto, not scored).** A real Wolfe wave only tests the 1-3 trendline once, at point 5 — but the channel has to hold on *both* sides, not just that one. The scanner checks every swing strictly between points 1 and 5 — including ones the combinatorial search skipped when picking a non-consecutive point selection — against both the 1-3 *and* the 2-4 boundary, using the same tolerance point 5 itself gets (`p5TolAtr`). If price already broke through either boundary earlier (e.g. a sharp plunge-and-recovery between points 3 and 4 on the 1-3 side, or a breach of the 2-4 side that never gets its own dedicated "test" the way point 5 tests 1-3), the pattern is rejected outright: the five chosen points can score well on their own while the actual price path between them already violated the channel the wave depends on.

**P5-Zone is gated, not just scored.** `P5-Zone` (15 points, table above) also hard-blocks `valid` now: a point 5 that overshoots the 1-3 boundary by several `p5TolAtr` multiples isn't testing the trendline, it's blowing through it — the same class of violation as a mid-pattern boundary break, just at the last point instead of an earlier one. Chart-confirmed case: a bear pattern's point 5 sat roughly a full price unit beyond the 1-3 line (several times the allowed tolerance) but still reached score 73 because other criteria made up the missing 15 points.

**Convergence (apex) is gated, not just scored.** `Convergence` (15 points, table above) also hard-blocks `valid` now: if the 1-3/2-4 wedge has already converged at or before point 5 (`etaOk` false), the pattern isn't a completed Wolfe wave — and drawing it anyway is actively misleading, since the fallback apex projection extrapolates the same already-converged slopes further out and can go wildly, even negatively, off in price. Chart-confirmed case: a bull pattern whose true apex sat essentially on top of point 5 still validated at score 72 and drew lines shooting off into empty space using a fabricated projection point far beyond where the lines had actually met.

## Invalidation

Once a pattern fires, its point-5 level (± 0.5 ATR, in the direction against the trade) is tracked every bar, independently of every other tracked pattern. Depending on `Invalidierung nur per Close` (default on), either a confirmed close beyond it or an intrabar high/low breach marks that specific pattern invalidated, draws a label, removes it from tracking (freeing a slot for a new one), and fires the `WWSP · INVALIDATED` alert.

## Multiple patterns

`Max. gleichzeitige Patterns` (default 5, capped at 8) caps how many valid patterns are tracked and drawn at once — each owns up to ~9 lines and 6 labels, so the cap also bounds drawing-object growth (`Suchbereich letzte Swings` is similarly capped at 24 to bound the combinatorial search cost). Each search pass finds the best-scoring valid combination *per point-5 anchor* in the window, not just the single overall best — so two non-overlapping Wolfe waves elsewhere on the chart both surface instead of only the higher-scoring one. Overlap is checked on the [point 4, point 5] completion leg only, not the full [point 1, point 5] span — since point 1 can sit anywhere within `Suchbereich letzte Swings`, two well-separated completions often still share the same early swings for points 1-2, and comparing the full range would let one wide pattern block every other candidate in that stretch. A margin — a third of the larger pattern's own total width — is added to both patterns' ranges before comparing, so two same-direction patterns crammed a few bars apart still count as overlapping even if their exact completion legs don't literally touch. Overlap only ever competes patterns of the *same direction* for a slot — a bull and a bear pattern occupying the same bars are two different scenarios, not rivals. If a new candidate's completion leg overlaps an already-tracked, same-direction pattern's, it only replaces it when it scores higher (and isn't the exact same pattern already tracked); otherwise it's skipped. After every search, a cleanup pass checks all tracked patterns pairwise and removes the lower-scoring one of any same-direction, overlapping pair still left over — this catches overlap chains (A overlaps B, B overlaps C, but A and C don't directly overlap) that the one-at-a-time insertion check alone can miss.

Once all `Max. gleichzeitige Patterns` slots are full, a new non-overlapping candidate evicts the currently weakest (lowest-scoring) tracked pattern if it scores higher — otherwise the first patterns to fire that happen not to invalidate for a while would permanently occupy every slot and silently block every better candidate found afterward. `Invalidierte/ersetzte Patterns behalten` controls whether a pattern's drawings are deleted once it's invalidated, replaced, or evicted, or kept on the chart as history.

## Debug

Enabling `Score-Breakdown Debug-Tabelle` shows a table with the current best-in-depth candidate's pass/fail state per criterion and its running score against `Mindestqualität` — useful for tuning thresholds without guessing why a candidate didn't fire. `Kandidat-Mindestscore (Watch)` controls the near-miss marker's lower bound.

Enabling `Pine-Logs aktivieren (Fehlersuche)` (off by default) emits `WWSP SWING`/`WWSP SEARCH`/`WWSP PATTERN FIRED`/`WWSP PATTERN REPLACED`/`WWSP PATTERN EVICTED`/`WWSP INVALIDATED` lines to the Pine Logs pane, including how many 5-point combinations were evaluated per search and the full per-criterion breakdown (including `boundaryHold`) of the single best-in-window candidate — useful when a symbol/timeframe combination seems to produce no signals at all, or to check why a specific candidate wasn't tracked (score too low, boundary broken prematurely, replaced by/skipped in favor of an overlapping higher-scoring one, or skipped because the cap was full and it didn't beat the weakest tracked pattern).
