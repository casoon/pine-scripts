## v2.4 — 2026-06-14
- **Setup-strength gate** (`minOppSignal`, default 40) — a signal only fires when its 4H opportunity clears this. Driven by the layer-test finding that opportunity ≥ 40 entries averaged **+1.47 R** while weaker setups averaged **−0.62 R** — the Setup layer is a strong, validated filter, so it is re-introduced as a gate (the stop-and-reverse still flips, just at a strong setup). The same layer test also found the Regime discriminates *inversely* — counter-trend +0.60 R beats with-trend −0.67 R (this is a reversal system; counter-trend is the edge). 0 disables the gate.
- **Signal stats panel** (`showStats`, on) — a live performance table (bottom-right) over the loaded history, split **Long / Short**: position count, win rate, average R, net R per closed stop-and-reverse position, and failed % (positions that hit the 1.2 ATR adverse mark). Lets the signal knobs be tuned against numbers directly on the 1H chart instead of by eye, and exposes the long-vs-short asymmetry (the counter-trend leg's drag). A second small table is a **layer test**: avg R by Regime alignment (with-trend vs counter-trend) and by Setup strength (opportunity ≥ 40 vs below), captured at entry. If a pair separates clearly, that layer is a real filter and earns its place; if not, it is dead weight. (Replaced the grade A/B/C breakdown, which the stats had already shown to be non-discriminating.) R is measured vs the fresh entry (re-entry averaging ignored — a slight approximation).
- **Entry confirmation (1 bar)** (`entryConfirmMode`: Off / All signals / Counter-trend only — **default All signals**, `confirmAtr`) — the direct failed-signal reducer: skips entries that immediately run against the trigger (the `held 0b` instant fails, ~40% of all failures). 'All signals' applies it symmetrically to both directions. — takes the entry the bar *after* the trigger, and only if price did not immediately run `confirmAtr` ATR (default 0.5) against the trigger price. 'Counter-trend only' applies it by **situation, not direction** — only to a signal opposing the daily regime (symmetric: confirms shorts in an uptrend, longs in a downtrend), so the disadvantaged side adapts to the market rather than hardcoding a long/short bias. Targets the dominant failure pattern the failed-debug revealed: instant-reversal entries (`held 0b`, several per window) that ran straight to the 1.2 ATR adverse mark — not the cross clusters originally hypothesised. Costs 1 bar of lag.
- **Cross handling: two configurable filters for the multi-cross / too-early problem** (both off by default, independent, combinable). (1) **Cross Settle** (`useCrossSettle`, `settleWindow`) — a WT cross only fires after a quiet window with no newer valid cross of the same type, so a cluster of crosses resolves to its *last* cross; this shifts a too-early entry forward to the cross that actually held (e.g. a cluster around 29.5 → fires on the later cross). (2) **Confirmation WT** (`useConfirmWt`, `confirmSmooth`) — a smoother WT1 (EMA of the trigger WT1) must agree in direction for a cross to count; its slope rejects the cluster micro-crosses the fast WT produces but the smooth one does not. Both apply only to the cross trigger (turn / velocity / exhaustion unaffected) and are validated against the failed-debug cross-count.
- **Failed-signal study tools** — `Mark Failed Signals` recolours a signal's tag (light blue by default, configurable) the moment price trades `failAtrMult` ATR (default 1.2 — the old stop distance) against its entry, so failed entries are visible at a glance. `Failed Debug Label` then prints a larger label at the failure with study values: the **WT cross count in the window before entry** (`x4 crosses in 10b` — high = choppy multi-cross zone = the 'too early' pattern), bars held, the loss in R, and the entry price/grade. Built to investigate why an entry fired too early (e.g. several WT crosses in a row) and whether the entry could be pushed later.
- **Removed: fixed TP/SL boxes** — the static risk/target boxes are gone (with `Show TP / SL Zones`). They contradicted the model: the exit is dynamic (the opposite signal / stop-and-reverse), so fixed ATR targets misrepresented it. The price chart now carries only the compact signal labels and the optional pivot connectors.
- **Declutter: compact signal labels** — new `Signal Label Detail` input (Compact / Full / None). Compact (default) prints a small tag (`L B·53`) below/above the bar; Full keeps the four-line detail; None shows only the pane triangle. The full breakdown is always in the label tooltip on hover. Labels are now decoupled from the TP/SL boxes (`Show TP / SL Zones`), so turning the boxes off leaves an unobstructed price view with just the compact tags.
- **Signal model: stop-and-reverse alternation** — signals now alternate by construction: a long closes any open short and a short closes any open long, so every short is eventually faced by a long and vice versa. The exit of a trade IS the opposite signal (the opposite WT trigger) — indicator-native, no external stop. Direction is enforced by the open position, not by the gates: the counter-trigger always fires, so the system can no longer spam one side while the provable counter-cross is silently filtered out. Grade / Quality / Opportunity become conviction labels (A/B/C on the signal) and no longer block a signal. `Min Signal Grade` removed; `Signal Cooldown` is now `Minimum Hold (bars)` — the minimum a position is held before the opposite trigger may reverse it (whipsaw guard). The old trend-health exit (regime-based, which opposed counter-trend reversal trades by design) and its exit-warning markers are removed; exit alerts now fire on the reverse ("Long/Short Closed"). This is the basis for the planned strategy conversion, which can measure the alternating entries/exits directly.
- **Signal model: setup-aligned reversals** (`maxWaitBars`, default 5) — after the minimum hold, a reverse first waits for a WT trigger that coincides with a setup (the high-opportunity zone) rather than firing on the first bare trigger, so the entry lands in a better location instead of where the trigger happens to lag. If no setup-aligned trigger appears within `maxWaitBars`, it falls back to any trigger so the alternation can't get stuck. 0 disables the preference. Addresses the observation that setup markers sat at better entries than the signals. A `Setup Grace (bars)` window (default 3) makes a trigger count as setup-aligned when a setup was active *now or within the last few bars* — so the common case of a setup forming first and a cross confirming it a few bars later still qualifies (the cross follows the setup).
- **Signal model: adverse-move re-entry** (`reentryOnAdverse`, default on) — the same-direction alternation lock is lifted when price trades against the open position past its entry (a long is undercut / a short is exceeded). A fresh same-side signal at a better price is then allowed, so a reversal that fired too early can be re-taken (averaged) instead of locking out until the opposite signal. Each re-entry resets the entry reference and the minimum-hold clock. **Tamed** by `Max Re-entries per Position` (default 2) and `Re-entry Quality Floor` (default 32): a re-entry only adds while under the count cap and while Quality holds at/above the floor — cutting off the runaway chains where each add scored lower than the last (e.g. Q 24→22→16). The first entry and the reverse are unaffected.
- **Removed: bias background band + dead inputs** — the green/red bias background shading added no visual value and is gone, along with its now-orphaned support code (`showBiasBand`, `biasConfirmBars`, the bias-confirm/info/tip chain). Also removed `useNetBias`, an input that was never wired to anything. The regime bias is still shown in the dashboard BIAS rows. (Input audit: all remaining 62 inputs are referenced.)
- **Timing: exhaustion reversal trigger** (`useExhaustion`, default on) — fires the bar WT1 hooks back from a deep extreme (`|WT1| ≥ exhaustLevel`, default 50): the sharp overbought/oversold turn at a major top or bottom. Built to catch big exhaustion reversals (e.g. the kind the predecessor's zero-lag Ultimate Smoother core caught but which the lagging JMA core detects too late) without going full zero-lag and flooding the mid-range — it only fires at the extremes, so it stays narrow. Still gated by setup downstream. **Grade bypass:** an exhaustion reversal grades B on context (with setup) without passing the trend-confirmation grade (regime/quality) — at a top every trend measure opposes the short and the direction-sensitive ER penalty drove Quality under the merit floor, so the grade was silently blocking the very signal the trigger fired. The deep-extreme reversal context is the edge, not trend confirmation. Dashboard WT-Trigger tooltip distinguishes it.
- **Quality score: finer discrimination** — three coarse components made continuous so the score separates signals instead of clustering them at the 45/55 grade edges: (1) **RS dead weight reclaimed** — with the RS filter off (default) its 15% no longer contributes a flat neutral 50 that lifted every score equally; the weight is handed to entropy (20→35%), which is direction-neutral. (Deliberately not to ER: ER is direction-penalised ×0.35 against the trend, so weighting it more would amplify the counter-trend penalty and starve longs in a downtrend / shorts in an uptrend — entropy keeps the reclaim symmetric.) (2) **Vol gradient** — the binary 75/35 ATR-band step replaced by a gradient peaking in the healthy band (≈0.9–1.4× the 20-bar ATR SMA) and falling toward dead/wild tape. (3) **Acceleration z-score** — the binary `slope5 > slope10` step replaced by the slope gap normalised by its own 50-bar stdev, so a strong momentum thrust scores higher than a marginal one. Overall score level is roughly preserved; re-check `gradeAQuality` / `meritQualMin` against the new Quality readout in the labels. The entry label now also carries a weighted quality breakdown line (`ER.. V.. A.. E..`, summing to Quality) so it is visible on a screenshot which component carries the score.
- **Grade: Timing + Opportunity merit path (with quality floor)** — Grade B is now also reachable on a signal's own merit: `Timing ≥ minTimingGrade` (default 55) AND `Opportunity ≥ meritOppMin` (default 40) AND `Quality ≥ meritQualMin` (default 45) — not only via regime alignment or `Quality ≥ 55`. Previously the A/B/C grade looked only at regime + quality, so in a non-aligned regime a long needed Quality ≥ 55 and a well-timed reversal with Quality 51 was killed as C. The merit path folds in the Timing + Opportunity stages the grade had been ignoring, while the Quality floor and the stronger Opportunity bar stop it from resurrecting low-quality, far-from-swing knife-catches (the pivot overlay confirmed an unfloored merit floods with those). Grade A unchanged (regime-aligned + Quality ≥ 55). New inputs: `Grade B Timing Merit ≥`, `Grade B Merit Opportunity ≥`, `Grade B Merit Quality Floor`.
- **Gate: opportunity threshold lowered** — `minOpportunity` default 50 → 35 (effective 25 after buffer). This unblocked the short side, whose 4H opportunity rarely cleared the old 40 gate. (`WT Cross Zone Limit` was briefly opened to 20 during testing but reverted to 0 — it showed no added signals, since mid-range crosses are still held back downstream by the grade rule.)
- **Timing: WT velocity trigger** (`useWtVelocity`, default **off** / opt-in) — fires on a sharp 1-bar WT1 slope reversal (`wtVel ≥ velMinSlope`, default 3) from a shallower zone (`velZone`, default 0 = below midline) after a confirmed falling/rising run. Catches fast, shallow turns the WT Turn misses (the Turn needs depth below `triggerTurnLevel`; Velocity needs speed). Independent of Trigger Mode, pivot-free. Off by default — showed no added signals in the baseline window; enable to test in other regimes. Dashboard WT-Trigger tooltip distinguishes cross / turn / velocity-turn.
- **Timing: earlier WT turn trigger** — the Cross-or-Turn turn now fires on the WT1 hook out of the extreme (after a confirmed falling/rising run of `turnFallBars` bars, default 2) instead of waiting for the histogram to turn. The histogram lags the WT1 low by 1-2 bars, so dropping that requirement lands turn signals closer to the actual swing. New `WT Turn Min Falling Bars` input controls strictness; `WT Turn Level` tooltip clarified. Pivot-free — verify the effect on the ΔLow readout in the entry labels.
- **Feature: Pivot reference markers (control overlay)** — confirmed swing highs/lows (`ta.pivothigh`/`ta.pivotlow`, configurable left/right bars) plotted on the price chart as small triangles at the actual pivot bar. Purely a control overlay for judging how close a signal landed to the real turn — it never influences which signals fire.
- **Feature: Pivot → signal distance** — dotted connector from the current swing extreme (swing low for long, swing high for short) to the entry, with bar/ATR distance and proximity printed in the signal label (`ΔLow 3b / 0.8R · P68`). Diagnostic only; small distance = signal fired close to the swing, so you can read entry accuracy straight off a screenshot.
- **Feature: diagnostic values in signal labels** — entry labels now show grade, pivot distance/proximity, a `⚠K` knife-catch flag (entry below the last confirmed swing low / above the high), and the Opportunity / Regime / Timing / Quality scores that produced the signal (`O62 R71 T80 Q58`) for screenshot-based accuracy review.
- **Feature: robust swing reference** — distance is measured against the *live* current-leg extreme (lowest low since the last confirmed pivot high / highest high since the last pivot low), so a fresh unconfirmed low is used instead of a stale confirmed pivot. ATR distance is always ≥ 0 by construction.
